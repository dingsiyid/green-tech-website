Projects & Case Studies

Germany Energy Transition

Energy

Germany has successfully increased renewable energy share to 45% through large-scale development, setting a global example for energy transition.

Germany 2010-2023

Project Details

Germany's Energy Transition (Energiewende) is a comprehensive energy policy reform aimed at phasing out nuclear and fossil fuels while transitioning to renewable energy sources.

Renewable Energy Share
45%
Annual Emission Reduction
120M tons
Key Measures:
  • Large-scale development of wind and solar power
  • Establishment of smart grids and energy storage systems
  • Improvement of building energy efficiency standards
  • Promotion of electric vehicles and public transport
Success Factors:

Through long-term stable policy support, technological innovation, and market mechanisms, Germany has successfully transformed its energy structure, providing replicable experience for other countries.

Singapore Garden City

Green City

Through vertical greening, rooftop gardens, and ecological corridors, Singapore has created a modern city where humans and nature coexist harmoniously.

Singapore 1965-Present

Project Details

Singapore's Garden City initiative began in 1965, aiming to transform Singapore into a "city in a garden" through systematic greening planning to enhance urban environmental quality.

Green Coverage Rate
47%
Number of Parks
350+
Key Measures:
  • Construction of national parks and nature reserves
  • Promotion of vertical greening and rooftop gardens
  • Establishment of ecological corridors connecting green spaces
  • Implementation of strict environmental regulations
Success Factors:

Through long-term planning, strict enforcement, and public participation, Singapore has successfully balanced urban development with ecological protection, becoming a model for sustainable cities worldwide.

Copenhagen Carbon Neutral City

Green City

Copenhagen plans to achieve carbon neutrality by 2025 through bicycle transportation, clean energy, and green buildings to promote urban sustainable development.

Copenhagen, Denmark 2009-2025

Project Details

Copenhagen's Carbon Neutral Plan is one of the world's most ambitious urban climate action plans, aiming to achieve carbon neutrality by 2025 through multi-sectoral collaborative efforts.

Bicycle Commuting Rate
45%
Emission Reduction Achieved
65%
Key Measures:
  • Construction of comprehensive bicycle infrastructure
  • Development of district heating systems
  • Promotion of offshore wind and biomass energy
  • Implementation of green building standards
Success Factors:

By integrating emission reduction measures in transportation, energy, and construction sectors, Copenhagen has created synergistic effects, providing a replicable carbon neutrality pathway for other cities.

China Three-North Shelterbelt

Eco Protection

The world's largest ecological project, effectively preventing desertification and improving regional ecological environment through afforestation.

Northern China 1978-2050

Project Details

The Three-North Shelterbelt Project is a large-scale ecological construction project implemented in northwest, north, and northeast China, aimed at preventing land desertification and improving ecological environment.

Afforestation Area
30M hectares
Forest Coverage Rate
13.6%
Key Measures:
  • Large-scale artificial afforestation and aerial seeding
  • Mountain closure for forest cultivation and grassland protection
  • Construction of farmland shelterbelt networks
  • Development of ecological economic forests
Success Factors:

Through long-term persistence, scientific planning, and public participation, the Three-North Shelterbelt Project has effectively curbed desertification expansion and improved regional ecological environment, providing a Chinese solution for global ecological governance.

Japan Circular Economy Model

Green City

Japan achieves maximum resource utilization and waste minimization through comprehensive waste classification and resource recycling systems.

Japan 2000-Present

Project Details

Resource Recovery Rate
85%
Waste Incineration Rate
80%

Netherlands Circular Agriculture

Eco Agriculture

Netherlands achieves perfect combination of high agricultural output and low environmental impact through precision farming technology and waste recycling.

Netherlands 2015-Present

Project Details

The Netherlands' circular agriculture model focuses on "closed-loop production," achieving coordinated development of agricultural production and environmental protection through technological innovation and system optimization.

Pesticide Use Reduction
60%
Water Use Efficiency
90%
Key Measures:
  • Development of precision irrigation and fertilization technologies
  • Promotion of greenhouse agriculture and vertical farming
  • Establishment of agricultural waste recycling systems
  • Implementation of ecological pest control
Success Factors:

Through technological innovation, knowledge sharing, and policy support, the Netherlands has successfully achieved a win-win situation of high agricultural yield and environmental protection, providing a demonstration for global sustainable agricultural development.
