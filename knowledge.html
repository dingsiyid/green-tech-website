Green Technology Knowledge Base

Clean Energy

Clean energy is a crucial pillar for achieving carbon neutrality, encompassing solar, wind, hydro, bioenergy, and geothermal power. Solar energy utilizes photovoltaic (PV) modules to directly convert sunlight into electricity, or employs solar thermal systems to concentrate heat for power generation. It has become one of the fastest-growing energy types globally. Wind energy converts the kinetic energy of air into electricity via wind turbines. Innovations like offshore wind farms and smart turbine technology are continuously boosting its efficiency. These renewable energy sources are progressively replacing fossil fuels, laying the foundation for a zero-carbon energy system.

Energy Storage & Electrification

Energy storage technology is a crucial link for ensuring the stable supply of clean energy. Electric vehicles (EVs) use high-energy-density batteries to power motors, significantly reducing carbon emissions in the transport sector. China currently leads the world in both the number of EVs in operation and its charging infrastructure. At the same time, the recycling of used batteries presents a new green challenge. Lithium batteries contain critical metals like nickel, cobalt, and lithium, which can be circulated back into the economy through recycling and reuse. The future goal is to establish a "full life-cycle green battery system," making energy use more efficient and sustainable.

Intelligent Agriculture

Smart agriculture utilizes information technology and intelligent equipment to enhance agricultural efficiency and ecological benefits. Drones perform precise spraying, seeding, and pest and disease monitoring, thereby reducing the use of chemical products. Vertical farms use artificial lighting, nutrient solutions, and climate control to enable year-round cultivation within urban areas. The integration of the Internet of Things (IoT) and Big Data monitoring makes agricultural production more scientific, efficient, and energy-saving, establishing it as a key direction for future food security and sustainable development.

Smart City

Smart cities represent the seamless integration of technology and sustainability. By combining smart grids, IoT systems, and artificial intelligence, they optimize energy distribution, transportation, and waste management. Smart grids balance renewable and traditional energy sources efficiently, while AI-powered waste systems automate sorting and recycling to minimize pollution. Data-driven infrastructure and sensors further enhance resource utilization, reduce carbon emissions, and improve residents' quality of life—making cities not only more efficient and connected but also greener and smarter for the future.

Green Manufacturing

Green manufacturing emphasizes reducing energy consumption and environmental pollution within industrial production. The circular economy model ensures resources continue to flow through the value chain via strategies like reuse, remanufacturing, and recycling. "Zero-emission factories" achieve near-zero pollution output by implementing energy consumption monitoring, exhaust gas recovery, and water resource recirculation. A growing number of enterprises are adopting green supply chains and smart manufacturing technologies, driving the transformation of Chinese industry toward a sustainable, high-value-added future.

Green Living

Green living begins with intentional individual action, promoting energy conservation, emission reduction, and responsible low-carbon consumption. Smart home systems can automatically regulate lighting, temperature, and appliance usage with precision, leading to significant reductions in overall energy consumption. "Carbon footprint" tools help citizens vividly understand their complete environmental impact across travel, diet, and daily shopping habits. Through low-carbon commuting, energy-efficient electricity use, and waste sorting, every person can contribute to a sustainable future. Technology makes environmental protection smarter and easier to achieve.

Environmental Protection

One of the key technologies for environmental protection is Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS). This technology captures carbon dioxide from industrial emissions and then either stores it deep underground or converts it into valuable reusable products like fuels and building materials. In high-emission sectors such as steel, cement, and thermal power, CCUS can significantly reduce carbon output, making it a critical tool for achieving "negative carbon emissions." It is rapidly becoming a major breakthrough area for Chinese energy transition and green innovation efforts.

Frontier Innovation

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly becoming a core driver of green technology. Artificial intelligence can predict energy demand, optimize power grid operation, identify pollution sources, and support ecological monitoring. In the monitoring of forests, oceans, and the atmosphere, AI combined with satellite and sensor technology can detect environmental anomalies in real-time. Furthermore, machine learning is accelerating the research and development of new materials, aiding breakthroughs in high-efficiency photovoltaics, batteries, and carbon capture technologies. AI makes green innovation smarter and more efficient.
